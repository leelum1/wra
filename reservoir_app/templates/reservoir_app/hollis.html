Hollis Reservoir

The Hollis reservoir was created by damming the Quare river, and was commissioned in 1936 by Sir Claude Hollis, a past Governor of Trinidad and Tobago, whom it was named after. It is fed by seven tributaries that flow directly into the resevoir: Waterfall, Crayfish, Inner Estate, Outer Estate, Mapapie, I can't remember the rest.

The blue intake tower that pulls water from the reservoir to send to the treatment plant below is accessible over a bridge. A nonfunctional Fuess guage is housed on the bridge, and was historically used to continuously monitor the lake level. Nowadays, a simple gauge board, which is painted onto the side of the intake tower, is read by the reservoir operators.

Several climatic stations are located on the western side of the dam. In addition, there is a rain gauge located across the lake near to the Crayfish tributary. Employees travel to the gauge via a boat to perform maintenance.

The ogee spillway at the south-western tip of the reservoir spills at a height of 620 ft. The water flows into a circular basin, which is a form of a labyrinth spillway, used to both dissipate energy and discharge as much water as possible. The water from the spillway is routed back to the Quare River.

Spillway Elevation 620 ft
Bottom Draw Off 575 ft
Maximum Depth 45 ft
Maximum Production 8.4 MGD
