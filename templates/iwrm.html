Integrated Water Resources Management

The Water Resources Agency of Trinidad and Tobago is responsible for the implementation of Integrated Water Resources Management (IWRM) throughout the country, in an effort to sustainably and effectively manage the country's water resources. IWRM is defined as:

A process which promotes the coordinated development and management of water, land and related resources in order to maximise economic and social welfare in an equitable manner without compromising the sustainability of vital ecosystems and the environment.

There are three sources of water in Trinidad and Tobago: Surface water (which include river intakes, springs, and water impounded by resevoirs): 60%, groundwater: 28%, and desalinated water: 12%. Unfortuantely, the majority of water that is treated and supplied by WASA is lost as Unaccounted-For-Water, which is water that is lost through leakage or irregular practices between entering a distribution system and reach the legitimate users. IWRM is needed to manage this water as WASA works to reduce the amount of water lost.

National IWRM Policy

The Water Resources Management Unit of the WRA, along with the Ministry of Public Utilities has developed a National IWRM Policy, that is to be used to guide the management of water resources in the country. The Policy was first published in 2005, and revised in 2017. A copy of this policy can be viewed here.

This policy provides guidelines on how the water resources of the country are to be used, which the Government of Trinidad and Tobago can refer to when legislation regarding water resources needs to be written or edited. Other topics included in the policy are abstraction licensing, ambient water quality standards, allocation prioritization, wastewater treatment, and flood management.

Adopt-A-River Programme

The Adopt-A-River Programme is an organisation that promotes the principles of IWRM. The objective of the program is:

To build awareness concerning issues impacting local watersheds and to facilitate the participation of public and private sector entities in sustainable and holistic projects aimed at improving the status of rivers and watersheds throughout Trinidad and Tobago

Visit their website to learn more.
